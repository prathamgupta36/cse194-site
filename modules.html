Module 1 Phone Phreaking Status: Coming soon
▾
The culture and techniques of phone phreaking, from blue boxes to late-era phreakers like Susan Thunder and the bridge into early hacking.
Assignment due: Sunday, February 8, 2026
Planned readings include “Secrets of the Little Blue Box” and “Searching for Susy Thunder”. Content and prompts coming soon.
Module 2 Early Hackers Status: Coming soon
▾
Early hacker culture: BBSes, IRC, Usenet, Kevin Mitnick, Tsutomu Shimomura, Loyd Blankenship and “The Hacker's Manifesto, and early policy moments.
Assignment due: Sunday, February 22, 2026
Planned readings include work on dial-up BBS culture, IRC history, Usenet, and Reagan-era policy inspired by WarGames. Details to be added.
Module 3 Hackers and Hacktivists Status: Coming soon
▾
From early worms to modern hacktivism: Robert Tappan Morris Jr., Dan Kaminsky, Aaron Swartz, and Phineas Fisher.
Assignment due: Sunday, March 8, 2026
Planned materials include reporting on the Morris worm, Kaminsky's DNS work, coverage of Aaron Swartz, and interviews/writings from Phineas Fisher.
Module 4 Hacking Collectives Status: Coming soon
▾
The rise of hacker groups and crews: Phrack, Legion of Doom, Masters of Deception, the Great Hacker War, Cult of the Dead Cow, l0pht, Shellphish, and more.
Assignment due: Sunday, March 22, 2026
Planned readings span Phrack profiles, historical writeups on LOD/MoD, Wired features, and talks/interviews on cDc and Shellphish.
Module 5 The Hacker Aesthetic Status: Coming soon
▾
How hacking is represented in film, TV, and internet culture: Sneakers, Hackers, Mr. Robot, and the language of “pwning”.
Assignment due: Sunday, April 5, 2026
Planned viewings include iconic hacker media plus readings on the origins and spread of “pwned” and related slang.
